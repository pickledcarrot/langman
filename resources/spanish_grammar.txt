Essential Spanish Grammar Review
===============================

Use this as a compact review sheet while you practice.

1. Articles and gender
- Nouns are usually masculine or feminine.
- Common pattern: nouns ending in -o are often masculine, and nouns ending in -a are often feminine.
- Use definite articles for "the": el, la, los, las.
- Use indefinite articles for "a/an": un, una, unos, unas.
- Adjectives often agree with the noun in gender and number.
- Example: el libro rojo, la casa roja, los libros rojos.

2. Singular and plural
- Add -s after a vowel: libro -> libros.
- Add -es after most consonants: papel -> papeles.
- If a noun or adjective ends in -z, change z to c before adding -es: luz -> luces.

3. Subject pronouns
- yo = I
- tu = you informal
- el/ella/usted = he/she/you formal
- nosotros/nosotras = we
- vosotros/vosotras = you all informal in Spain
- ellos/ellas/ustedes = they / you all
- Spanish often drops the subject pronoun when the verb already makes it clear.

4. Present tense basics
- Regular verb endings:
- -ar: hablo, hablas, habla, hablamos, hablan
- -er: como, comes, come, comemos, comen
- -ir: vivo, vives, vive, vivimos, viven
- Learn a few core irregulars early: ser, estar, ir, tener, hacer.

5. Ser vs estar
- ser is used for identity, origin, characteristics, time, and relationships.
- estar is used for location, temporary states, and many feelings or conditions.
- Example: Soy estudiante. Estoy cansado. Madrid esta en Espana.

6. Hay vs estar
- hay means "there is" or "there are".
- estar says where something is located.
- Example: Hay un libro en la mesa. El libro esta en la mesa.

7. Basic question words
- que = what
- quien = who
- donde = where
- cuando = when
- por que = why
- como = how
- cual = which / what one

8. Gustar pattern
- gustar works like "is pleasing to."
- me gusta = I like
- te gusta = you like
- le gusta = he/she/you formal likes
- nos gusta = we like
- les gusta = they/you all like
- Use gusta with a singular noun or infinitive, and gustan with plural nouns.
- Example: Me gusta leer. Me gustan los libros.

9. Negation
- Put no before the verb.
- Example: No entiendo. No quiero cafe.
- Words like nunca, nadie, and nada can also appear with no before the verb in many sentences.

10. Adjective agreement
- Adjectives usually match the noun in gender and number.
- Example: un chico alto, una chica alta, chicos altos, chicas altas.

11. Possessives
- mi, tu, su, nuestro, vuestro
- They also agree in number when needed: mis libros, nuestras casas.
- su can mean his, her, your formal, their, or your plural, so context matters.

12. Direct object pronouns
- me, te, lo, la, nos, os, los, las
- They replace the noun receiving the action.
- Example: Compro el libro -> Lo compro.

13. Indirect object pronouns
- me, te, le, nos, os, les
- They show to whom or for whom something is done.
- Example: Doy el libro a Ana -> Le doy el libro.

14. Common present irregulars
- tener: tengo
- hacer: hago
- poner: pongo
- salir: salgo
- venir: vengo
- decir: digo
- oir: oigo
- conocer: conozco

15. Stem-changing verbs
- e -> ie: pensar -> pienso
- o -> ue: poder -> puedo
- e -> i: pedir -> pido
- Usually the change happens in all present-tense forms except nosotros and vosotros.

16. Reflexive verbs
- Use reflexive pronouns: me, te, se, nos, os, se.
- Example: me levanto, se llama, nos acostamos.
- These are common for daily routines.

17. Near future
- ir + a + infinitive expresses "going to".
- Example: Voy a estudiar. Vamos a comer.

18. Simple past overview
- Preterite is for completed actions.
- Imperfect is for background, repeated past actions, age, time, and ongoing situations.
- Example: Ayer estudie dos horas. Cuando era nino, estudiaba en casa.

19. Por vs para
- por often expresses cause, exchange, movement through, duration, or means.
- para often expresses purpose, destination, deadline, or recipient.
- Example: Estudio para aprender. Gracias por ayudar.

20. Personal a
- Use a before a specific human direct object.
- Example: Veo a Maria. Conozco a mi profesor.

21. Comparisons
- mas ... que = more ... than
- menos ... que = less ... than
- tan ... como = as ... as
- mejor and peor are common irregular comparisons.

22. Commands overview
- Informal tu commands are very common: habla, come, vive.
- Negative tu commands use the present subjunctive: no hables, no comas, no vivas.
- Learn polite commands later after the basics are stable.

23. Practical study habits
- Track repeated mistakes by topic, not just by score.
- If you miss three items on the same topic, review that rule and write two original examples.
- Say answers out loud to connect grammar, spelling, and pronunciation.
